Declared as one of the most dangerous mountains, the Annapurna Mountain Range is located east of a great gorge cut through the Himalayas by the Kali Gandaki River. This series of peaks in the Himalayas stretches for 55 kilometers with Annapurna I as its highest point, standing at 8091 meters.

If you’re curious enough to see the breathtaking Annapurna Mountain Range, you better head to Sarangkot. Be aware that the place is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Nepal so expect to join crowds.

Located north of Kathmandu, Gosainkunda is a glacier lake that is mostly visited compared to the other glacier lakes connected to it.

Narayani River (what they mostly call it in southern Nepal) is more known as Kali Gandaki or Gandaki River and also known as Krishna Gandaki.

Kala Patthar is a small peak (not really considered as a mountain) with a name that literally means “black rock”.

Located in the remote area of Dolpo, trekkers can view more of Nepal’s amazing natural attractions by visiting Phoksundo Lake.

Another viewpoint worth visiting besides Kala Patthar is Poon Hill. Throughout the journey, you’ll be able to visit several villages with different cultures.
